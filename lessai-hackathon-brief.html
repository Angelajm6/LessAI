Top 3 hackathon matches — July to December 2026

1
AI Genesis 2026 — lablab.ai
Oct 26 – Nov 3, 2026  ·  Hybrid (online + Dubai finale)
Global 3,000+ builders Enterprise AI focus
86% fit
Real-world impact AI integration depth Business value Uniqueness

Judges explicitly score on practical business value and AI integration depth — both are LessAI's core identity. The global AI builder audience is your ideal early adopter. Live demo at the Dubai finale is a major credibility moment. Prior winners have included B2B SaaS productivity tools.

Action items
1
Choose a partner tech challenge. AI Genesis requires building with a sponsor's API/stack. Map OpenRouter → Anthropic Claude track; this unlocks the Claude partner prize pool on top of the main prize.
2
Frame the impact numerically. Judges want to see "X% productivity lift" or "saves Y hours/week." Run a quick 5-user test before the hackathon, collect time-on-task data.
3
Build a 90-second live demo path. Show: blank slate → onboarding → AI generates role-specific tasks → Prompt Lab scores a real prompt → progress streak. No slide deck needed — this alone wins.
4
Add an enterprise angle. Admin dashboard + team leaderboard is your team-tier story. Quantify: "Managers can see which team members haven't practiced ChatGPT this week." That's a retention/ROI hook.
2
Future of Work Hackathon — Devpost
Rolling submissions  ·  Virtual
Virtual Workforce skills Social impact track
82% fit
Worker empowerment Skill discovery Practical problem solving

The stated goal is "empower workers to discover, learn and solve practical industry challenges" — LessAI is literally the answer to this brief. The social impact track rewards tools that address workforce skill gaps, which AI literacy clearly does. Low competition vs. the big open AI events.

Action items
1
Lead with the equity angle. "AI skill gaps disproportionately affect non-technical workers who already use ChatGPT but never learned to prompt it well — LessAI closes that gap in 2 minutes/day." This is the social impact thesis.
2
Add a free-forever tier story. If LessAI has a genuinely free tier for individual workers (not just a trial), this framing directly answers the inclusion/access criterion judges will score.
3
Prepare a 5-min presentation + 3-min Q&A. This hackathon uses a live pitch format. Judges are from academia and investment — emphasize data (user cohort, completion rates, prompt score improvement %) over tech.
3
Automation Innovation Hackathon 2026 — Devpost
Dates TBA  ·  Virtual
Virtual Workflow automation Business solutions
74% fit
Automation depth Real-world application Business ROI

Theme is "Automate to Innovate: Build Solutions That Work." LessAI automates the most manual part of AI adoption — figuring out what to learn and how to practice. The weekly digest cron, streak automation, and AI-generated task stack are all genuine automation stories. Slightly lower fit because judges may expect pure workflow tools (Zapier-style), not edtech.

Action items
1
Re-frame as "AI adoption automation." Don't pitch LessAI as an education tool here — pitch it as "the automated AI onboarding layer your company never had." Position the AI path generation + weekly digest as a zero-touch automation pipeline.
2
Show the admin automation stack. Invite flow → auto-onboarding → auto-generated stack → weekly digest → streak reminders. That's a 5-step no-touch automation chain that runs without HR involvement.
3
Add one integration hook before submitting. A Slack notification when a teammate completes their daily task, or a Zapier webhook output, would make this feel like a true automation platform rather than a standalone app.

Universal gaps to close before any submission

Must-haves
1
60-second demo video. Record the full flow: onboard → tasks → Prompt Lab → score improvement. Judges watch videos first — this is your first impression.
2
One hard metric. "Users who complete the Prompt Lab see an average +X point quality improvement." Pull this from real usage data.
3
Live demo URL. lessai.io must be publicly accessible with a test account judges can use without signing up.
Nice-to-haves
4
Stripe billing page. Any hackathon with a "market potential" criterion will score higher if you can show a live payment flow — even a mock checkout page signals commercial viability.
5
2-3 beta users with quotes. A real testimonial from a non-technical worker ("I went from avoiding ChatGPT to using it daily") beats any slide. A CSM, HR manager, or sales rep quote is ideal.
